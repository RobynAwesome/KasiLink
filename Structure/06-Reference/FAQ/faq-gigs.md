# Jobs and Gigs - KasiLink Info
> Source: `public-works.pdf`, `government-programmes_projects-and-campaigns.pdf`, `the-Presidency.pdf` | Relevant to: Gig categories, home feed, about page

## Q: What is the Expanded Public Works Programme?
**A:** It is a government programme that creates work opportunities through labour-intensive projects. KasiLink can surface those opportunities so township residents can find them more easily.

## Q: Who is EPWP meant to help?
**A:** The programme is designed to create opportunities for unemployed and vulnerable people, with a focus on youth, women, and rural communities. It is a good fit for people looking for short-term work and skills exposure.

## Q: Why would KasiLink mention government job programmes?
**A:** Because KasiLink is meant to complement public employment efforts, not replace them. Government programmes and community gigs can sit side by side in one place.

## Q: What kinds of work does the government use for public works?
**A:** The source material describes labour-intensive work across infrastructure, environment and culture, non-state, and social sectors. That means the jobs can range from practical field work to community support work.

## Q: Is there a link between national priorities and local job access?
**A:** Yes. The Presidency’s planning documents frame job creation, youth opportunity, and reducing inequality as national priorities. KasiLink helps those priorities reach people at street level.

## Q: Can these gigs help someone build experience?
**A:** Yes. Public works programmes often combine income support with skills exposure and work experience. That makes them useful stepping stones for people entering the labour market.

## Q: What should I check before applying for an EPWP opportunity?
**A:** Verify that the opportunity comes from an official government source or a trusted municipal channel. Fraudulent EPWP adverts do circulate, so residents should confirm before sharing personal information.
