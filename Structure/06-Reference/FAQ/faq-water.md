# Water and Sanitation - KasiLink Info
> Source: `civic-services.pdf`, `local-government.pdf`, `water-and-sanitation.pdf` | Relevant to: Water alerts, utility schedule, community status

## Q: Who is responsible for water and sanitation services?
**A:** Municipalities deliver day-to-day services such as water and sanitation, while the national Department of Water and Sanitation sets the broader mandate and supports delivery. KasiLink should point residents to the right local authority first.

## Q: Are poor households supposed to pay for basic water and sanitation?
**A:** Government provides free basic municipal services to poor households at no charge, where those services are available. The minimum package can include water, sanitation, and electricity needed for basic living.

## Q: What should I do if my area has a water problem?
**A:** Contact your local municipality or the relevant water services authority. Local government is the first place residents should report service interruptions or request assistance.

## Q: Why does KasiLink track water alerts?
**A:** Water outages affect dignity, health, and daily work. Community alerts help residents see problems early and respond together.

## Q: What do the national water authorities actually do?
**A:** The Department of Water and Sanitation protects, manages, develops, conserves, and regulates water resources, and it supports effective water supply and sanitation delivery. Water boards help municipalities with bulk water and infrastructure support.

## Q: How does water service delivery connect to township life?
**A:** Water delivery is part of local service delivery, so disruptions affect homes, businesses, and small work opportunities. KasiLink’s utility features help make that visible in one place.

## Q: Why does the community status page include water information?
**A:** Because water service problems are part of the daily reality residents deal with. The status page helps connect water, safety, and load-shedding information so people can plan around disruptions.
