# Utility Disruption Rationale
> Sources: `load-shedding-context.md`, `service-directory.md`, `civic-services.pdf`, `local-government.pdf`
> Use in app: `/water-outages`, `/community-status`, `/utility-schedule`, utility-related trust copy

## Direct facts

- Presidential reporting states load-shedding dropped from 329 days in 2024 to 13 days in 2025, but municipal distribution issues remain a real local problem.
- Local municipalities remain responsible for core basic services such as water, sanitation, and related service delivery.
- Municipal debt and local service failure still threaten reliability even when national generation conditions improve.

## Product interpretation

- Utility-aware coordination is a core differentiator for KasiLink, not a side feature.
- Community status, water alerts, and utility schedule pages should help people judge whether work, travel, and household planning are safe and realistic for the next few hours.
