## What Changed

- National Energy Action Plan (2022) initiated the turnaround
- National Electricity Crisis Committee (NECOM) coordinated government and private sector
- Government-Business Partnership model: energy, transport/logistics, crime, youth unemployment workstreams
- Electricity Regulation Amendment Act: allows Eskom to restructure into separate generation, transmission, and distribution units
- Private sector investment now permitted in electricity market

---

## Remaining Challenges

- Municipal debt arrears grew 27% since previous financial year
- Many municipalities not paying Eskom — threatens Eskom's financial viability
- Grid expansion still required — 14,000km new transmission lines over 10 years
- Coal-fired plant reliability improved but ageing infrastructure remains a risk

---

## What This Means for KasiLink

**The load-shedding widget must be honest:**
- 2025 was dramatically better than 2024 (13 days vs 329 days)
- But "stable" ≠ "solved" — municipal failures can still cause localised outages
- Users in areas with municipal debt problems may still experience outages regardless of Eskom's national improvement

**How Grok should answer load-shedding questions:**
- "Is load-shedding over?" → Not entirely. Nationally it has reduced dramatically. Local municipal issues remain.
- "Why does my area still have outages?" → Could be municipal distribution issues, not Eskom generation
- "When will it end?" → Eskom is profitable again, plans significant infrastructure investment — trajectory is positive

**Community status and water alerts:**
- Link outages to municipal billing issues where relevant
- Distinguish between Eskom generation outages and municipal distribution outages
- The 329→13 days improvement is the most credible, citable SA energy fact available
