# Service Directory - KasiLink Info
> Source: `cooperative-Governance-and-Traditional-Affairs.pdf`, `the-Presidency.pdf`, `local-government.pdf`, `civic-services.pdf` | Relevant to: Community status, water alerts, utility schedule, incidents

## Municipal services
**What it is:** Local municipalities deliver water, electricity, sanitation, and related basic services.
**Why it matters:** Residents should start here when they need service delivery help or want to report a local issue.

## Ward councillor / ward committee
**What it is:** The ward councillor is the local political link between the municipality and the community.
**Why it matters:** It is one of the most direct routes for raising service delivery problems in a township.

## Co-operative governance
**What it is:** The cooperative governance system coordinates national, provincial, and local government so services work together.
**Why it matters:** KasiLink’s community status tools make more sense when users understand that service issues often span multiple government layers.

## Expanded Public Works Programme (EPWP)
**What it is:** A government work-opportunity programme that uses labour-intensive projects across infrastructure, environment, non-state, and social sectors.
**Why it matters:** It is one of the clearest examples of public-sector work opportunities that KasiLink can help surface.

## Government programmes and campaigns
**What it is:** A catalogue of employment, education, health, youth, and social development initiatives.
**Why it matters:** This is the broadest reference point for residents looking for support programmes beyond private-sector gigs.

## The Presidency
**What it is:** The centre of government that helps set national priorities and policy direction.
**Why it matters:** Its planning documents frame job creation, equality, and youth opportunity as national priorities that local platforms should support.
