## The Youth Skills Challenge

| Metric | Verified figure |
|--------|----------------|
| Root cause of youth unemployment | Skills mismatch — especially in digital sector and AI |
| Government response | R800 million allocated to National Skills Fund (2025) for digital/tech skills |
| Funding model | Pay-for-performance — payment linked to employment outcomes, not just training completion |

## Key Programmes

### Presidential Youth Employment Intervention (PYEI)
- Tests pay-for-performance model: employers pay for training only when it leads to employment
- Creates pathway from training to earning
- National Pathway Management Network links multiple sectors
- Includes revitalised national youth service component

### Higher Health Civic and Soft Skills Programme
- NQF Level 5, accredited by QCTO
- First programme of its kind in Africa
- Only SA programme aligned with all 17 UN Sustainable Development Goals
- Skills covered: decision making, emotional intelligence, social entrepreneurship, mental health, time management
- Hosted through public universities and TVET colleges (via Department of Higher Education and Training)

### National Skills Fund
- R800 million allocation (2025) specifically for digital and technology sector
- Focus on AI skills gap — recognised as critical to employment future
- Demand-led approach: skills training designed around actual market demand

## What This Means for KasiLink

- The tutoring feature directly supports this ecosystem — township learners building skills = KasiLink users
- Gig categories should include digital skills gigs (content creation, data entry, WhatsApp business support)
- The skills gap in AI/digital is an opportunity: KasiLink could surface PYEI and Higher Health opportunities
- 156,587 PYEI participants = proven demand for structured pathway into gig/formal work
- Grok AI should know about PYEI — users may ask "how do I get work experience?" and Grok can reference official programmes

---

## Framing KasiLink's Role

KasiLink is not a jobs board — it is a pathway platform. Users may start with informal gigs while building skills through programmes like PYEI or Higher Health. KasiLink surfaces both the immediate gig and the longer-term skill. That is the township-first value proposition.
