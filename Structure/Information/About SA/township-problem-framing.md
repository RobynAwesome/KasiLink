# Township Problem Framing
> Sources: `public-works.pdf`, `budget-accelerate-our-economic-and-social-recovery.pdf`, `south-Africas-economic-progress-in-2025_Signs-of-recovery-and-global-relevance.pdf`
> Use in app: homepage framing, recruiter/judge explanation, marketplace positioning, investor summary

## Direct facts

- South Africa is in a gradual recovery phase, with 0.5% GDP growth in Q3 2025 and a primary budget surplus of R48.9 billion.
- Government reports more than 11 million indigent households receiving free basic services support.
- Government reports 3.5 million EPWP work opportunities already created, with 4.4 million additional opportunities targeted in the MTEF period.
- Government continues to frame unemployment as a structural challenge even while recovery indicators improve.

## Product interpretation

- Recovery at the national level does not remove the township-level need for hyperlocal work discovery.
- KasiLink should be framed as a proximity and coordination layer that complements public work and recovery programmes instead of competing with them.
- The product should consistently present itself as township-first work and services infrastructure, not a generic job board.
